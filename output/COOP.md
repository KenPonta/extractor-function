# Cooperative Education School of Engineering King Mongkut's Institute of Technology Ladkrabang

“Cooperative Education” refers to an educational system that provides teaching and learning in an educational institution alternating with direct experience of working at a systematic company with cooperation from the company and all parties involved It is an educational system that combines learning with performance (Working Integrated Learning: WIL).

Co-operative education curriculum based on fundamental learning principles that coordinate. (Experiential Learning) with the principle of learning in real conditions (Authentic Learning), which is integrated education management with work is a case of standard-level learning that provides students with the opportunity to practice skills, knowledge, and specific skills relevant to the profession, Practical knowledge to solve problems in the job and training are most readily available upon graduation.

### Objective

- 1. To prepare students in Career Development with skills and readiness to enter the work system.
- 2. To enhance academic, professional, and personal development skills valuable than an internship.
- 3. To provide opportunities for establishments in both the public and private sectors to participate in the development of graduate quality.
- 4. The development of modern teaching and learning curriculum meets standards and meets the needs of the labor market.
- 5. To build relationships between enterprises and educational institutions through cooperative education students and supervisory faculty. This will lead to better and more extensive cooperation.


Duration of work 1st semester, starting from July - October (including working time of at least 16 weeks)

Benefits

- 1. Student

- • Increase professional experience in the field of study
- • Increase personal development. More self-confidence
- • Have the opportunity to be offered a job before graduation.
- • Choose the right career path.
- • Be a potential graduate.


- 2. Educational institutions


- • Academic cooperation and good relationship with graduates.
- • Get information back for improving the course teaching.
- • Educational institutions recognized by the labor market.


- 3. Establishment
- • Reduce production costs, able to use cooperative education in recruiting employees. This reduces the risk of ineffective recruiting of new employees.
- • Has established academic cooperation with educational institutions.
- • Receive tax incentives under 2 laws:


- 1.Section 33 (1) of the Skill Development Promotion Act B.E.
- 2. Section 5 of the Royal Decree Issued under the Revenue Code Regarding Tax Exemption (No. 437) B.E.


2548 (issued to support Section 33 (1)

Of the Skill Development Promotion Act, BE 2545) In addition, the establishment will receive tax benefits. (2 times deductible) by donating money or property to state educational institutions under 2 laws:

- 2.1. Royal Decree Issued under the Revenue Code Regarding Tax Exemption (No. 420) B.E.
- 2.2. Royal Decree Issued under the Revenue Code Regarding Tax Exemption (No. 476) B.E. 2551


### Preparation of establishments that accept cooperative students.

Preparation of the establishment before accepting cooperative education students perform planning and setting procedures, detailed as follows:

- 1. Planning for the admission of cooperative education students. Establishment should hold a meeting between the administrators, personnel staff and head of the student admissions department. In which all departments may write on the topic of the work that students to perform and the reviewer to consider before giving the head of the department to the school to announce to the students.
- 2. The establishment should allocate budget and resources such as compensation, welfare, equipment, tools and appliances suitable for the performance of students. And is safe for the performance of students, etc.
- 3. The head of the department who wants to accept students to work must assign personnel in the department to act as supervisors. The supervisor must have sufficient time for counseling and operation to be completed regarded as the workload given by the establishment to perform the same as regular work.
- 4. The job description that is suitable for the performance of cooperative education should be a job that cooperative students can complete within the time. Should not beyond the ability of a student to do the actual job maybe a job at the planned workplace. But those who work still do not have time to work. The developer should plan and set a time frame for students to work as scheduled.

- 5. Supervisor at the establishment and the person responsible for the operation Cooperative education work should jointly consider the selection of students who notify their intention from the educational institution. By selecting students who are qualified for the job description or project topic set by the establishment.
